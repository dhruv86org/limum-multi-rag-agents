# Leave Policies and Procedures

## Overview

TechCorp provides comprehensive leave benefits to support employee well-being, family needs, and life events. This document outlines all leave types, eligibility requirements, and request procedures.

## Paid Time Off (PTO)

### PTO Accrual
PTO accrues based on tenure:

**Years 0-2:**
- 15 days (120 hours) per year
- 10 hours accrued per month
- 0.462 hours per pay period

**Years 3-5:**
- 20 days (160 hours) per year
- 13.33 hours accrued per month
- 0.615 hours per pay period

**Years 6+:**
- 25 days (200 hours) per year
- 16.67 hours accrued per month
- 0.769 hours per pay period

### PTO Usage Guidelines
- Minimum increment: 4 hours (half day)
- Must be requested in advance via HR portal
- Manager approval required
- Blackout periods may apply for critical business periods
- Denied requests can be appealed to HR

### PTO Carryover Policy
- Maximum carryover: 40 hours to next calendar year
- Excess PTO automatically paid out in December
- Carryover hours must be used by March 31
- No carryover for employees on PIP

### PTO Payout
- Unused PTO paid out upon termination
- Payout rate: Current base salary rate
- Maximum payout: 200 hours
- Payment included in final paycheck

## Sick Leave

### Sick Leave Allocation
- 10 days (80 hours) per year
- Provided on January 1st (or start date)
- Does not accrue monthly
- Cannot be carried over to next year
- No payout upon termination

### Sick Leave Usage
Can be used for:
- Personal illness or injury
- Medical appointments
- Care for immediate family member
- Mental health days
- Preventive care

### Documentation Requirements
- 1-2 consecutive days: No documentation required
- 3-5 consecutive days: Doctor's note required
- 6+ consecutive days: FMLA paperwork may be needed
- Recurring absences: Medical certification may be requested

### Notification Procedure
1. Notify manager before shift start
2. Log sick time in HR portal
3. Provide expected return date
4. Update manager if absence extends

## Parental Leave

### Birth Mother Leave
- 16 weeks (640 hours) paid leave
- Can start up to 2 weeks before due date
- Must be taken consecutively
- Job protection guaranteed
- Benefits continue during leave

### Non-Birth Parent Leave
- 8 weeks (320 hours) paid leave
- Can be taken within 12 months of birth
- May be split into two 4-week periods
- Includes same-sex partners
- Applies to fathers and adoptive parents

### Adoption Leave
- 8 weeks (320 hours) paid leave
- Available for domestic and international adoption
- Leave begins when child is placed
- Legal documentation required
- Same benefits as parental leave

### Parental Leave Process
1. Notify HR and manager as soon as possible
2. Submit leave request 30 days before start date
3. Complete parental leave application
4. Coordinate with HR on leave schedule
5. Arrange coverage with manager

### Return to Work
- Guaranteed same or equivalent position
- No penalty for taking leave
- Optional phase-back schedule (2-4 weeks)
- Lactation support provided
- Flexible schedule available for first month

## Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

### FMLA Eligibility
Must meet all criteria:
- Employed for 12+ months
- Worked 1,250+ hours in last 12 months
- Work site has 50+ employees within 75 miles

### FMLA Qualifying Reasons
- Birth or adoption of child
- Care for spouse, child, or parent with serious health condition
- Employee's own serious health condition
- Military family leave
- Military caregiver leave

### FMLA Duration
- Up to 12 weeks per 12-month period
- 26 weeks for military caregiver leave
- Can be taken intermittently or continuously
- Runs concurrently with other leave types

### FMLA Process
1. Notify HR within 30 days (or ASAP for emergencies)
2. Complete FMLA application
3. Provide medical certification
4. Recertification may be required
5. Periodic status updates to HR

### Job Protection
- Same or equivalent position upon return
- Benefits maintained during leave
- Accrual of seniority continues
- No retaliation for taking FMLA

## Bereavement Leave

### Immediate Family
For death of spouse, child, parent, sibling:
- 5 days (40 hours) paid leave
- May request additional unpaid time
- Must be used within 30 days of death

### Extended Family
For death of grandparent, grandchild, in-law, aunt, uncle:
- 3 days (24 hours) paid leave
- Additional time can use PTO
- Must be used within 30 days of death

### Bereavement Process
1. Notify manager immediately
2. Submit bereavement leave request
3. Provide death certificate (if requested)
4. Coordinate coverage with team

## Jury Duty Leave

### Policy
- Fully paid leave for jury service
- No limit on duration
- Job protection guaranteed
- Must return to work if dismissed early

### Procedure
1. Provide jury summons to manager and HR
2. Submit leave request when dates confirmed
3. Provide documentation of service
4. Return jury duty payment to company (if required by state)

## Military Leave

### Military Service
- Unpaid leave for military service
- Job protection under USERRA
- Benefits may continue (case-by-case)
- Reemployment rights protected

### Military Training
- Up to 2 weeks paid leave per year
- Applies to National Guard and Reserve
- Requires advance notice
- Provide training orders to HR

### Military Spouse Leave
- Unpaid leave for deployment-related events
- Covered under FMLA in some cases
- Flexible scheduling available

## Sabbatical Leave

### Eligibility
- 5+ years of continuous employment
- Good performance standing
- Manager and VP approval required

### Sabbatical Duration
- 4 weeks (160 hours) paid sabbatical
- Additional unpaid time can be requested
- Must be taken within 12 months of eligibility

### Sabbatical Purpose
Encouraged for:
- International travel
- Volunteer work
- Personal development
- Family time
- Creative pursuits

### Sabbatical Process
1. Submit proposal 6 months in advance
2. Outline sabbatical purpose and goals
3. Plan coverage with team
4. Manager and VP approval
5. Share learnings upon return

## Leave of Absence (LOA)

### Personal Leave of Absence
- Unpaid leave for personal reasons
- Duration: 30 days to 6 months
- No guarantee of same position
- Benefits may be suspended
- Requires senior management approval

### Medical Leave of Absence
- Beyond FMLA or for non-FMLA eligible employees
- Requires medical documentation
- Duration based on medical need
- Benefits continuation negotiable
- Reasonable accommodation considered

### Educational Leave
- Leave for full-time study
- Unpaid, up to 12 months
- Must be relevant to role or career path
- Return commitment required
- May be partially funded

### LOA Process
1. Submit LOA request 60 days in advance
2. Provide justification and documentation
3. HR review and approval
4. Sign LOA agreement
5. Arrange coverage and transition plan

## Holidays

### Observed Holidays (11 per year)
- New Year's Day (January 1)
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day (3rd Monday in January)
- Presidents' Day (3rd Monday in February)
- Memorial Day (Last Monday in May)
- Independence Day (July 4)
- Labor Day (1st Monday in September)
- Thanksgiving Day (4th Thursday in November)
- Day After Thanksgiving (4th Friday in November)
- Christmas Eve (December 24)
- Christmas Day (December 25)
- New Year's Eve (December 31)

### Holiday Pay Policy
- All full-time employees receive paid holidays
- Part-time employees receive pro-rated pay
- If holiday falls on weekend, observed on Friday/Monday
- Must be employed before and after holiday to receive pay

### Working on Holidays
If required to work on company holiday:
- 1.5x regular pay for non-exempt employees
- Comp time (time-and-a-half) for exempt employees
- Must be pre-approved by manager

### Floating Holidays
- 2 floating holidays per year
- Can be used for religious or cultural observances
- Must be requested 1 week in advance
- Expire at end of calendar year

## Voting Leave

### Policy
- Paid time off to vote in elections
- Up to 2 hours for local/state elections
- Up to 3 hours for federal elections
- Must be used on election day
- Proof of voting may be requested

## Volunteer Time Off (VTO)

### Policy
- 2 days (16 hours) paid VTO per year
- For approved charitable activities
- Individual or company-organized events
- Must provide proof of volunteer service
- Cannot be carried over

### Qualifying Activities
- 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
- Community service projects
- Disaster relief efforts
- Educational programs
- Environmental conservation

### VTO Process
1. Identify qualifying volunteer opportunity
2. Submit VTO request with organization details
3. Manager approval required
4. Complete volunteer service
5. Submit verification of service

## Leave Request Procedures

### Standard Process
1. Check leave balance in HR portal
2. Discuss timing with manager
3. Submit request via HR system
4. Await manager approval
5. Receive confirmation email

### Approval Timeline
- PTO: 3 business days for approval
- Sick leave: Report same day
- Other leave: 5-10 business days

### Denial and Appeals
If request denied:
- Manager must provide reason
- Employee can request reconsideration
- Alternative dates may be suggested
- Appeal to HR if dispute continues

### Emergency Leave
For unexpected situations:
- Contact manager immediately
- Submit request as soon as possible
- Retroactive approval may be granted
- Documentation may be required

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I take PTO during my first 90 days?**
A: PTO accrues from day one, but we recommend waiting until after probation for extended time off.

**Q: What happens to my PTO if I'm terminated?**
A: Unused PTO up to 200 hours is paid out in your final paycheck.

**Q: Can I donate my PTO to a coworker?**
A: We don't currently have a PTO donation program, but you can contribute to our emergency relief fund.

**Q: Do I need a doctor's note for one sick day?**
A: No, documentation is only required for 3+ consecutive sick days.

**Q: Can I take FMLA intermittently?**
A: Yes, for qualifying conditions you can take FMLA in increments as small as 1 hour.

**Q: What if I need more than 12 weeks of medical leave?**
A: You may request a personal leave of absence or explore disability options with HR.

**Q: Can parental leave be extended?**
A: You can use accrued PTO or request unpaid leave to extend parental leave.

**Q: Do benefits continue during unpaid leave?**
A: It depends on the type of leave. Contact HR for specific benefit continuation policies.
