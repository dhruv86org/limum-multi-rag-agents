# Expense Reimbursement Policy

## Overview

This policy outlines TechCorp's expense reimbursement procedures, approval requirements, and spending guidelines. All employees must comply with this policy when incurring business expenses.

## General Principles

### Eligible Expenses

Business expenses must be:
- **Necessary**: Required for business purposes
- **Reasonable**: Appropriate cost for the item/service
- **Properly documented**: Receipts and business justification provided
- **Timely submitted**: Within 30 days of incurrence
- **Pre-approved**: For expenses over designated thresholds

### Personal Responsibility

Employees are expected to:
- Exercise good judgment when spending company funds
- Seek most economical options while maintaining quality
- Use corporate discounts and preferred vendors when available
- Avoid personal expenses on corporate cards
- Report suspected policy violations

## Expense Categories

### Travel Expenses

**Air Travel**

Domestic Flights:
- Economy class for flights under 5 hours
- Premium economy for flights 5+ hours (with approval)
- Book at least 14 days in advance when possible
- Use corporate travel portal for best rates
- Maximum cost: $500 domestic, $1,500 international

International Flights:
- Economy class for flights under 6 hours
- Premium economy for flights 6+ hours
- Business class for flights 10+ hours (VP approval required)
- Book at least 21 days in advance
- Maximum cost: $2,500

**Baggage Fees:**
- One checked bag reimbursed
- Additional bags require approval
- Premium seat selection not reimbursed
- Wi-Fi on flights over 3 hours (max $20)

**Accommodations**

Hotel Guidelines:
- Book through corporate travel portal
- Select hotels within reasonable distance of work location
- Prefer corporate-rate hotels

Maximum nightly rates:
- Tier 1 cities (NYC, SF, LA): $250
- Tier 2 cities: $175
- Tier 3 cities: $125
- Extended stays (7+ nights): Negotiate weekly rates

**Alternative Accommodations:**
- Airbnb allowed with manager approval
- Must provide itemized receipt
- Cleaning fees reimbursed
- Service fees reimbursed
- No luxury properties

**Hotel Incidentals:**
- Reimbursable: Internet, parking, business center
- Not reimbursable: Movies, minibar, spa, gym

**Ground Transportation**

Approved Methods:
- Uber/Lyft for local transportation
- Rental cars for multi-day trips or remote locations
- Public transportation encouraged
- Parking fees at hotels and airports
- Tolls and mileage (if using personal vehicle)

Rental Cars:
- Economy or mid-size vehicles only
- Book through corporate travel portal
- Decline insurance (covered by corporate policy)
- Refuel before returning
- Premium/luxury vehicles require VP approval

**Personal Vehicle Mileage:**
- Reimbursed at IRS standard rate (currently $0.655/mile)
- Must log starting/ending odometer readings
- Submit mileage log with expense report
- Not reimbursable for normal commute

**Parking and Tolls:**
- Airport parking reimbursed
- Hotel parking reimbursed
- Tolls during business travel reimbursed
- Parking tickets NOT reimbursed

### Meals and Entertainment

**Meal Allowances**

Daily limits (per person):
- Breakfast: $20
- Lunch: $25
- Dinner: $50
- Daily maximum: $75

**Meal Guidelines:**
- Receipts required for meals over $25
- Alcohol limit: 2 drinks per meal, $15 per drink
- No alcohol without food
- Tip: 15-20% (included in meal cost)

**Business Meals**

Client/prospect meals:
- Requires manager approval for groups 5+
- Document attendees and business purpose
- Submit itemized receipt
- Reasonable locations (no ultra-luxury)

Team meals:
- Quarterly team dinners approved ($75/person)
- Manager approval required
- Celebrate milestones and achievements
- No team meals during conferences (covered separately)

**Entertainment Expenses**

Approved entertainment:
- Client entertainment events
- Tickets to sporting events/shows (with clients)
- Golf outings (with clients)
- Conference social events

Requirements:
- Pre-approval from VP required
- Clear business purpose documented
- Client names and companies listed
- Maximum: $200 per person

Not reimbursable:
- Employee-only entertainment
- Spouses/partners (unless client's spouse attending)
- Personal entertainment during travel

### Office and Supplies

**Office Supplies**
- Pens, paper, notebooks: Reimbursed
- Desk accessories: Up to $50/quarter
- Order through approved vendors when possible
- Used equipment available (check first)

**Technology Equipment**

Approved purchases (with IT approval):
- Monitor: Up to $400
- Keyboard/mouse: Up to $100
- Headset: Up to $150
- Webcam: Up to $100
- Laptop stand: Up to $50
- Docking station: Up to $200

**Home Office Setup**

One-time stipend for remote employees:
- Desk: Up to $400
- Chair: Up to $400
- Lighting: Up to $100
- Total maximum: $900

Recurring expenses:
- Internet: $50/month
- Phone: $75/month
- Electricity: Not reimbursed

### Professional Development

**Training and Courses**

Annual learning budget: $2,000 per employee

Eligible expenses:
- Online courses (Udemy, Coursera, etc.)
- Professional certifications
- Industry conferences
- Technical books
- Workshop fees

Requirements:
- Manager pre-approval required
- Must be relevant to role or career development
- Certificate of completion for courses
- Share learnings with team

**Conferences**

Approved conferences (with manager approval):
- Registration fees
- Travel and accommodations (per travel policy)
- Meals (per meal allowance)
- Networking events

Conference guidelines:
- Maximum 2 conferences per year
- Submit justification and expected value
- Book early bird rates when available
- Share key takeaways with team

**Certifications**

Fully reimbursed certifications:
- AWS/Azure/GCP cloud certifications
- PMP/Agile project management
- Industry-specific credentials
- Language certifications (if job-related)

Requirements:
- Pre-approval required
- Must pass exam for reimbursement
- Maintain active certification
- 1-year employment commitment after certification

**Books and Subscriptions**

Approved:
- Technical books: Up to $50/month
- Industry publications: Up to $100/year
- Professional memberships: Up to $200/year
- Online learning platforms: Within annual budget

Submit via expense system:
- Receipt required
- Brief description of relevance
- Manager approval for subscriptions over $100/year

### Telecommunications

**Mobile Phone**

Company-provided phone:
- Standard plan included
- No personal reimbursement needed

Personal phone for business:
- $75/month reimbursement
- Submit monthly expense
- No receipt required for standard amount

**Internet**

Remote employees:
- $50/month reimbursement
- Must have minimum 50 Mbps speed
- Submit monthly expense
- Receipt required for reimbursement

**Software and Services**

Approved business tools:
- Slack, Zoom (company-wide licenses)
- GitHub, AWS (team licenses)
- Productivity tools (case-by-case)

Requirements:
- IT security review required
- Manager and IT approval
- Annual renewal review

## Corporate Credit Cards

### Card Issuance

**Eligibility:**
- Managers and above: Automatic
- Individual contributors: As needed basis
- Travel-heavy roles: Upon request

**Card limits:**
- Standard employee: $5,000
- Manager: $10,000
- Director: $25,000
- VP and above: $50,000

### Card Usage

**Approved uses:**
- Business travel expenses
- Client entertainment
- Business supplies and materials
- Software and subscriptions (pre-approved)
- Conference registrations

**Prohibited uses:**
- Personal purchases
- Cash advances
- Gift cards (except approved client gifts)
- Cryptocurrency
- Gambling

### Card Reconciliation

**Monthly requirements:**
- Submit expense report by 5th of following month
- Attach receipts for all transactions
- Categorize each expense correctly
- Provide business justification

**Consequences of late submission:**
- 1st offense: Warning
- 2nd offense: Card suspended
- 3rd offense: Card revoked

## Expense Submission Process

### Required Documentation

**Receipts:**
- Itemized receipt required for expenses over $25
- Credit card statement alone not sufficient
- Receipt must show: vendor, date, items, amounts
- Digital photos of receipts acceptable

**Expense Report:**
- Submitted via Expensify
- Categorized correctly
- Business purpose explained
- Attendees listed (for group expenses)

**Special documentation:**
- Mileage log (for personal vehicle use)
- Client names (for entertainment)
- Conference agenda (for conference travel)
- Flight itinerary (for air travel)

### Submission Timeline

**Standard timeline:**
- Submit within 30 days of expense date
- Late submissions require director approval
- Expenses over 90 days: Generally not reimbursed

**Reimbursement processing:**
- Approved expenses: 5-7 business days
- Direct deposit to registered bank account
- Check by mail (if no direct deposit setup)

### Approval Requirements

**Standard expenses (under $500):**
- Manager approval only
- Auto-approved if within policy

**Large expenses ($500-$2,500):**
- Manager approval
- Finance review
- May require additional justification

**Major expenses (over $2,500):**
- Manager approval
- Director/VP approval
- CFO approval
- Pre-approval required

**Foreign transaction fees:**

- Daily max: $100

**Asia (major cities):**
- Breakfast: $20

### Visas and Vaccinations

**Reimbursable:**
- Business visa fees
- Expedited processing (if needed for travel timeline)
- Required vaccinations for travel

**Not reimbursable:**

- No receipts required

## Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are NOT reimbursed:

**Personal expenses:**
- Personal meals (not during travel)
- Personal entertainment
- Gym memberships
- Personal grooming
- Clothing (except required uniforms/PPE)

**Travel-related:**
- Travel insurance (covered by corporate policy)
- Airline club memberships
- Premium seat upgrades
- Hotel early check-in/late checkout fees
- Convenience fees when avoidable

**Other:**
- Traffic/parking violations
- Lost luggage (file claim with airline)
- Personal phone bill (except approved stipend)
- Home internet (except approved stipend)
- Pet care during travel

## Policy Violations

### Consequences

**Minor violations:**
- First offense: Warning and coaching
- Second offense: Written warning
- Expense may be denied

**Serious violations:**
- Immediate denial of expense
- Suspension or revocation of corporate card
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Legal action for fraud

**Examples of serious violations:**
- Submitting personal expenses as business
- Falsifying receipts
- Duplicate submissions
- Claiming expenses never incurred

## FAQs

**Q: What if I lost a receipt?**
A: Submit expense with explanation. Small amounts (<$25) may be approved without receipt. Frequent missing receipts will be flagged.

**Q: Can I expense alcohol?**
A: Yes, during business meals only. Limit 2 drinks per meal, $15 per drink. No alcohol purchases without food.

**Q: What if a flight/hotel is over the limit due to last-minute booking?**
A: Submit with explanation. Emergency/last-minute travel may be approved with manager justification.

**Q: Can I combine personal and business travel?**
A: Yes, but only business portion reimbursed. Must clearly separate expenses. Extra personal days not covered.

**Q: What if client insists on expensive restaurant?**
A: Use judgment. Document situation in expense report. Generally approved if client-driven.

**Q: Can family join me on business trip?**
A: Personal guests allowed, but no company reimbursement for their expenses. Your expenses must match what you'd spend alone.

**Q: What about tips?**
A: 15-20% tip included in meal/transportation costs. Excessive tipping not reimbursed.

**Q: How do I expense a team lunch?**
A: Submit with list of attendees and business purpose. Manager approval required. Within meal allowance per person.
