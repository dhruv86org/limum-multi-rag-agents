# Procurement and Vendor Management Policy

## Overview

This policy establishes TechCorp's procurement procedures, vendor selection criteria, and contract management requirements. The goal is to ensure cost-effective purchasing while maintaining quality, compliance, and risk management.

## Procurement Principles

### Core Principles

**Value for Money:**
- Obtain best total cost of ownership, not just lowest price
- Consider quality, service, reliability, and total cost
- Evaluate lifecycle costs, not just purchase price

**Fair Competition:**
- Provide equal opportunity to qualified vendors
- Transparent and objective selection process
- No conflicts of interest
- Competitive bidding for significant purchases

**Risk Management:**
- Vendor due diligence and background checks
- Contract terms protect company interests
- Business continuity and disaster recovery
- Information security and data protection

**Compliance:**
- Adherence to legal and regulatory requirements
- Ethical business practices
- Anti-corruption and anti-bribery
- Environmental and social responsibility

**Efficiency:**
- Streamlined approval processes
- Preferred vendor relationships
- Standardization where appropriate
- Leverage company buying power

## Approval Authority

### Purchase Approval Limits

**Individual Contributors:**
- Up to $500: Manager approval
- $500 - $2,500: Director approval
- Over $2,500: Not authorized

**Managers:**
- Up to $2,500: Self-approval
- $2,500 - $10,000: Director approval
- Over $10,000: VP approval

**Directors:**
- Up to $10,000: Self-approval
- $10,000 - $50,000: VP approval
- Over $50,000: CFO approval

**VPs:**
- Up to $50,000: Self-approval
- $50,000 - $250,000: CFO approval
- Over $250,000: CEO approval

**CFO:**
- Up to $250,000: Self-approval (with CEO notification)
- Over $250,000: CEO + Board approval

### Contract Authority

**Standard Agreements (using approved templates):**
- Same limits as purchase approval above

**Non-Standard Agreements:**
- All require Legal review
- Add one approval level higher
- CFO approval for any unlimited liability clauses

**Multi-Year Commitments:**
- Automatically require CFO approval
- Board approval for commitments >$500k total

## Procurement Process

### Step 1: Needs Assessment

**Identify Requirements:**
- What problem are you solving?
- What are must-have vs. nice-to-have features?
- What is the budget?
- What is the timeline?
- Who are the stakeholders?

**Business Case:**
For purchases over $10,000:
- Problem statement
- Proposed solution
- Alternatives considered
- Cost-benefit analysis
- ROI calculation
- Risk assessment

**Stakeholder Approval:**
- IT approval for technology purchases
- Security approval for SaaS/cloud services
- Legal approval for significant contracts
- Finance approval for budget allocation

### Step 2: Vendor Research

**Identify Potential Vendors:**
- Preferred vendor list
- Industry research
- Peer recommendations
- Analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester)
- Online reviews

**Initial Screening:**
- Meet basic requirements
- Financial stability
- Industry experience
- Customer references
- Security and compliance certifications

**Shortlist:**
- Narrow to 3-5 vendors
- Request information packets
- Schedule demos/presentations
- Obtain initial pricing

### Step 3: Request for Proposal (RFP)

**When to Use RFP:**
- Purchases over $50,000
- Complex requirements
- Multiple qualified vendors
- Custom solutions
- Long-term relationships

**RFP Contents:**
```
1. Company Overview
2. Project Background
3. Scope of Work
4. Technical Requirements
5. Functional Requirements
6. Integration Requirements
7. Security and Compliance Requirements
8. Service Level Expectations
9. Support and Training Needs
10. Pricing and Payment Terms
11. Evaluation Criteria
12. Timeline and Milestones
13. Submission Instructions
14. Terms and Conditions
```

**RFP Timeline:**
- Day 0: RFP issued
- Day 7: Vendor questions due
- Day 10: Answers published
- Day 21: Proposals due
- Day 28: Initial evaluation complete
- Day 35: Vendor presentations
- Day 42: Final selection

### Step 4: Vendor Evaluation

**Evaluation Criteria:**

**Technical (30%):**
- Meets functional requirements
- Integration capabilities
- Scalability and performance
- Technology stack
- Innovation and roadmap

**Financial (25%):**
- Total cost of ownership
- Pricing structure
- Payment terms
- Hidden costs
- Value for money

**Experience (20%):**
- Industry experience
- Similar customer success stories
- Company stability
- Market position
- References

**Support and Service (15%):**
- Implementation support
- Training offered
- Ongoing support availability
- SLA commitments
- Account management

**Risk and Compliance (10%):**
- Security certifications
- Data protection
- Compliance (SOC 2, GDPR, etc.)
- Insurance coverage
- Business continuity

**Scoring Matrix:**
```
Criteria          Weight  Vendor A  Vendor B  Vendor C
Technical           30%       25        28        22
Financial           25%       22        20        24
Experience          20%       18        19        17
Support/Service     15%       13        14        12
Risk/Compliance     10%        9         9        10
---------------------------------------------------
Total Score        100%       87        90        85

Recommendation: Vendor B
```

### Step 5: Negotiation

**Negotiation Strategy:**

**Pricing:**
- Request volume discounts
- Multi-year pricing locks
- Most favored customer clauses
- Annual price increase caps (3-5%)
- Right to competitive pricing reviews

**Terms:**
- Payment terms (Net 30 preferred)
- Cancellation provisions
- Renewal terms and notice periods
- Exit assistance obligations
- IP ownership clarity

**Service Levels:**
- Uptime guarantees (99.9% standard)
- Response time commitments
- Resolution time targets
- Penalties for SLA breaches
- Performance credits

**Contract Length:**
- Start with shorter term (1-2 years)
- Option to extend
- Avoid long lock-ins
- Include performance review clauses

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Know your BATNA (Best Alternative)
- Don't show excessive eagerness
- Bundle items for better discounts
- Request custom package pricing
- Get everything in writing

### Step 6: Due Diligence

**Financial Due Diligence:**
- Credit check
- Financial statements review
- D&B report
- Confirm business insurance
- Payment history with others

**Security Due Diligence:**
- SOC 2 Type II report
- Penetration test results
- Security questionnaire
- Data processing agreement
- Incident response procedures

**Legal Due Diligence:**
- Corporate registration verification
- Legal disputes search
- Regulatory compliance
- Ownership structure
- Related parties disclosure

**Reference Checks:**
- Contact 3-5 customer references
- Ask about: reliability, support, value, issues
- Check online reviews
- Industry reputation
- Former customer feedback

### Step 7: Contract Execution

**Contract Review:**
- Legal team review
- Markup and negotiation
- Final approval from authorized signatory
- Execute via DocuSign
- Store in contract management system

**Implementation Planning:**
- Kickoff meeting scheduled
- Project plan created
- Resources assigned
- Timeline confirmed
- Success criteria defined

## Vendor Management

### Vendor Onboarding

**New Vendor Setup:**
1. Complete vendor registration form
2. Provide W-9 and business license
3. Vendor added to accounting system
4. Payment terms configured
5. Primary contacts assigned
6. Vendor portal access granted

**Vendor Classifications:**

**Tier 1 - Strategic:**
- Revenue >$250k annually
- Mission-critical services
- Long-term partnership
- Executive sponsor assigned
- Quarterly business reviews

**Tier 2 - Preferred:**
- Revenue $50k - $250k
- Important services
- Ongoing relationship
- Department owner assigned
- Semi-annual reviews

**Tier 3 - Standard:**
- Revenue <$50k
- Tactical purchases
- Transactional relationship
- As-needed management
- Annual review

- Monitor financial health

- Credit rating changes
- Market news and reports
- Contingency planning

**Security Risk:**
- Annual security reviews
- Penetration test reports

- Access reviews

## Contract Management

### Contract Repository

**Contract Storage:**
- Central repository in SharePoint
- Organized by vendor and category
- Access controls by role
- Version control
- Search functionality

**Contract Metadata:**
- Vendor name and contact
- Contract type and category
- Start and end dates
- Auto-renewal terms
- Notice period
- Value and payment terms
- Assigned owner
- Key terms summary

### Contract Lifecycle

**Pre-Renewal (90 days before expiration):**
- Review performance
- Evaluate continued need
- Assess market alternatives
- Decide: renew, renegotiate, or replace

**Pre-Renewal (60 days):**
- Initiate renegotiation if continuing
- Issue RFP if replacing
- Provide cancellation notice if terminating

**Pre-Renewal (30 days):**
- Finalize new terms
- Execute new contract
- Update systems and records

**Post-Renewal:**
- Communicate changes to stakeholders
- Update budget if pricing changed
- Schedule next review

### Contract Amendments

**When Amendments Needed:**
- Scope changes
- Pricing adjustments
- Term extensions
- Service level changes
- Additional users or licenses

**Amendment Process:**
1. Request amendment from vendor
2. Negotiate terms
3. Legal review
4. Appropriate approval (per limits above)
5. Execute amendment
6. Update contract repository
7. Communicate to stakeholders

## Procurement Compliance

### Ethical Procurement

**Prohibited Practices:**
- Accepting gifts over $100 value
- Conflicts of interest
- Kickbacks or bribes
- Favoritism without justification
- Misuse of confidential information
- Personal benefits from vendors

**Gifts and Entertainment:**
- Modest meals allowed (<$75/person)
- Reasonable entertainment (sports, events) allowed
- Gifts under $100 acceptable
- Anything over $100 must be reported
- Lavish or frequent gifts declined

**Conflicts of Interest:**
Must disclose if:
- Family member works for vendor
- Financial interest in vendor company
- Outside business relationship
- Any personal benefit from selection

### Audit and Compliance

**Internal Audits:**
- Random sampling of purchases
- Contract compliance reviews
- Vendor due diligence verification
- Approval authority compliance
- Documentation completeness

**External Audits:**
- Annual financial audit includes procurement
- SOC 2 audit covers vendor management
- Industry-specific compliance audits

**Record Retention:**
- Contracts: 7 years after expiration
- Purchase orders: 7 years
- Invoices: 7 years
- Vendor evaluations: 3 years
- Correspondence: 3 years

## Emergency Procurement

### When to Use

Emergency procurement allowed for:
- System outages threatening business
- Security incidents requiring immediate response
- Regulatory deadlines
- Natural disasters or crises

**Emergency Authority:**
- VP or above can approve emergency purchases
- Must notify CFO within 24 hours
- Retroactive documentation required
- Post-incident review conducted

### Process

1. Document emergency situation
2. Obtain verbal approval from VP/CFO
3. Make necessary purchase
4. Submit formal request within 48 hours
5. Complete retroactive paperwork
6. Review in next procurement meeting

## Vendor Termination

### Termination Reasons

**Performance Issues:**
- Repeated SLA breaches
- Unresolved quality problems
- Poor customer service
- Lack of responsiveness

**Business Changes:**
- Vendor acquired or merged
- Strategic direction change
- Product discontinued
- Pricing no longer competitive
- Better alternative available

**Compliance or Risk:**
- Security breach
- Regulatory violations
- Financial instability
- Ethical concerns
- Contract violations

### Termination Process

**30-60 Days Before Termination:**
1. Review contract termination clauses
2. Identify replacement vendor
3. Plan transition
4. Notify vendor of non-renewal/termination
5. Request transition assistance

**During Transition:**
1. Data export and validation
2. Knowledge transfer
3. Parallel run if critical
4. User training on new solution
5. Cutover execution

**Post-Termination:**
1. Verify data deletion
2. Revoke access
3. Final invoice review and payment
4. Lessons learned documentation
5. Update vendor records
